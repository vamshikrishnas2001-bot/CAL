⚠️ REMEMBER
You can survive 3 minutes without air, 3 hours without shelter in harsh weather, 3 days without water, 3 weeks without food.
🌲
LOST IN FOREST / JUNGLE
▶
1
STOP walking immediately. Sit down, breathe slowly. Most people die because they panic and walk deeper into forest.
2
Look around for landmarks — hills, rivers, roads, power lines. These lead to civilization.
3
Follow water downstream. Rivers always lead to villages or roads eventually.
4
Make noise every 10 minutes — shout, whistle, bang rocks together. Rescuers listen for sounds.
5
Create a signal — make a large X or SOS on open ground using rocks/sticks visible from air.
6
Stay in one place after sunset. Moving in dark forest is very dangerous. Build a fire instead.
⚠️ DIRECTION TRICK
Find north without compass — at night, find the North Star (brightest star in small group). During day, a stick shadow points west to east. Moss grows on the north side of trees in India.
🚫 NEVER DO THIS
Never eat unknown berries or plants. Never drink river water directly. Never run from a wild animal — back away slowly. Never separate from your group.
⛰️
LOST IN MOUNTAINS
▶
1
Never go down steep slopes. More people die going down than up. Find a ridge and follow it.
2
Watch for weather changes. Dark clouds mean storm. Seek shelter before rain starts — hypothermia kills fast.
3
Build wind shelter — sit behind large rocks or dig into snow if available. Wind steals body heat rapidly.
4
Signal rescuers with mirror or phone screen. Flash sunlight towards helicopters in groups of 3.
5
Conserve energy. Move slowly, breathe deeply. High altitude means less oxygen — you tire 3x faster.
⚠️ ALTITUDE WARNING
Symptoms of altitude sickness: headache, nausea, dizziness. Go DOWN immediately if you feel these — do not sleep at same altitude.
💧
FINDING CLEAN WATER
▶
1
Collect rainwater in any container — your shirt, leaves shaped into funnel, helmet, plastic bag.
2
Follow animal tracks downhill — they always lead to water. Listen for sound of flowing water.
3
Morning dew — wipe grass and leaves with your shirt early morning, then wring it out to drink.
4
Boil all water for at least 1 minute before drinking. Use any metal container over fire.
5
Plants with water — coconut, bamboo stems contain water. Cut banana stem base for clean water.
6
Dig near dry riverbeds — water is often 1-2 feet underground where river used to flow.
🚫 NEVER DRINK
Never drink urine. Never drink seawater. Never drink stagnant green water. Never drink directly from river without boiling.
🏕️
BUILDING EMERGENCY SHELTER
▶
1
Find a dry location — avoid valleys (flood risk), hilltops (wind/lightning), near dead trees (falling risk).
2
Lean-to shelter — lean large branches against a fallen tree. Cover with smaller branches, leaves, bark.
3
Insulate the floor — pile dry leaves 6 inches deep to sleep on. Cold ground steals body heat fast.
4
Make shelter small — a small shelter traps your body heat better than a large one.
5
Face shelter entrance away from wind and towards your signal fire for maximum warmth.
⚠️ QUICK SHELTER TIP
A large plastic bag or emergency blanket can be a complete shelter. Always carry one when trekking. Your car is also an excellent shelter — stay with it, it's easier to find.
🔥
MAKING FIRE WITHOUT LIGHTER
▶
1
Collect dry tinder first — dry leaves, dry grass, birch bark, dead plant fluff. Must be completely dry.
2
Use phone battery — touch both ends of a battery to steel wool or foil from a gum wrapper. It will spark.
3
Friction method — carve a notch in flat dry wood. Spin a dry stick fast in the notch until coal forms. Drop coal into tinder bundle and blow gently.
4
Lens method — use phone screen glass, glasses lens, or water in a clear plastic bag to focus sunlight on tinder.
5
Build fire in a triangle — small tinder in center, medium sticks around it, large logs outside. Never smother it with too much wood at once.
⚠️ SIGNAL FIRE TIP
Add green leaves or wet wood to make white smoke (visible in forests). Add rubber or plastic for black smoke (visible in snow/open areas). 3 fires in triangle = international distress signal.
🌊
FLOOD / HEAVY RAIN
▶
1
Move to high ground immediately. Do not wait to see how bad it gets. Flash floods happen in minutes.
2
Never walk in moving water above your ankles. 6 inches of fast water can knock you down. 2 feet can sweep away a car.
3
Stay away from drains, rivers, bridges. These overflow without warning and current is deadly.
4
Make a flotation device — sealed empty bottles, sealed bag of air, jerrycans all float. Hold tight.
5
If swept away — float on your back feet first, protect your head. Grab any fixed object — tree, post, wall.
🚫 FLOOD DANGERS
Never drive through flooded roads — engines die, then car floats. Flood water contains sewage, chemicals, live electricity from downed wires. Snakes escape floods — watch where you step.
🚒
FIRE EMERGENCY
▶
1
Get OUT first, call 101 outside. Never go back inside a burning building for anything.
2
Crawl low under smoke. Smoke rises — clean air is 12-18 inches from the floor. Crawl to exit.
3
Feel the door before opening. If door is hot — DO NOT open it. Find another way out or seal the gap.
4
Seal gaps under doors with wet clothes/towels to keep smoke out. Signal from window.
5
If clothes catch fire — STOP, DROP, ROLL. Do not run — it fans the flames. Roll on the ground to smother fire.
6
If trapped above ground — go to the roof or a window. Signal with a bright cloth. Prepare to jump only as absolute last resort.
⚠️ SMOKE KILLS FASTER THAN FIRE
Most fire deaths are from smoke inhalation, not burns. A wet cloth over nose and mouth filters some smoke. Move fast — you may have only 2-3 minutes before smoke is deadly.
🐍
SNAKES & WILD ANIMALS
▶
1
Snake bite — stay calm. Panic speeds venom through blood. Sit still, keep bitten limb BELOW heart level.
2
Do NOT suck the venom, cut the wound, apply tourniquet, or use ice. These all make it worse.
3
Remember the snake's appearance for doctors — color, head shape, pattern. Take a photo from safe distance if possible.
4
Get to hospital within 4 hours. Remove watches/rings from bitten limb — swelling will come.
5
Wild animal encounter — stand tall, make noise, back away slowly. Never turn and run. Make yourself look bigger.
6
Elephant encounter — if it mock charges, stand still and make noise. If it really charges, run and hide behind a large tree.

BASIC FIRST AID
▶
1
Heavy bleeding — press hard with cloth for 10 minutes without removing. If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top. Do not lift.
2
Broken bone — do not try to straighten it. Immobilize with sticks tied with cloth. Keep patient still and warm.
3
Choking — 5 back blows between shoulder blades, then 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich). Repeat until object comes out.
4
Unconscious person — place in recovery position (on their side). Tilt head back to open airway. Check breathing every 2 minutes.
5
Burns — cool with running water for 20 minutes. Do not use ice, butter, or toothpaste. Cover loosely with clean cloth.
6
Dehydration signs — dark urine, dizziness, dry mouth, confusion. Drink water with a pinch of salt and sugar if available.
7
CPR (no breathing, no pulse) — 30 chest compressions (push hard, 2 inches deep, fast), then 2 rescue breaths. Repeat until help arrives.
📵
NO PHONE / BATTERY DEAD
▶
1
Signal with mirror or shiny object — even a phone screen or metal can be seen 10km away in sunlight. Flash in groups of 3.
2
Universal distress signal — any signal in groups of 3 means help needed. 3 whistle blasts, 3 gunshots, 3 fires, 3 shouts. Wait 1 min, repeat.
3
Create ground signals visible from air — SOS or X shape using rocks, logs, or anything contrasting. Make them at least 10 feet wide.
4
Bright colors attract attention — hang bright clothing high in a tree. Move it to wave when you hear aircraft or vehicles.
5
Smoke signals — maintain a smoky fire. Green leaves = white smoke. Rubber/plastic = black smoke. Keep fire burning day and night.
6
Leave a trail if you must move — break branches, stack rocks in arrow shape pointing your direction of travel.
💡 SAVE BATTERY TIPS
Turn on airplane mode to save battery. Turn off WiFi, Bluetooth, screen brightness. SMS uses much less battery than calls. Even with 1% battery you can send a location SMS.
